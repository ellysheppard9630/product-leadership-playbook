# Executive Review

Executive Review is a recurring leadership meeting that provides visibility into the health of the Product portfolio and the value being delivered to the business.

Unlike Delivery Sync, this meeting is not focused on active projects.

Unlike Quarterly Planning, it is not about deciding future investments.

Executive Review demonstrates how current investments are performing, identifies organizational risks, and provides leadership with the information needed to make strategic decisions.

---

# Purpose

Executive Review answers one question:

> **Are we investing in the right work, and is it creating measurable business value?**

The objective is to provide leadership with clarity—not every project detail.

Executives should leave understanding:

- Where investments are succeeding
- Where intervention is needed
- What decisions require leadership support

---

# Ownership

Executive Review is owned by Product Leadership.

Engineering Leadership participates as a strategic partner, particularly when discussing delivery health, platform investments, and organizational risks.

The meeting represents a unified view of Product and Engineering—not two separate status reports.

---

# Objectives

During each Executive Review the team should:

- Review strategic initiatives
- Review delivery health
- Highlight customer outcomes
- Discuss major risks
- Review engineering investments
- Surface executive decisions
- Confirm organizational priorities remain aligned

The meeting should help executives make informed investment decisions.

---

# Typical Attendees

Required

- CTO
- VP of Product
- Director of Product
- Engineering Leadership

Recommended

- Director of Engineering
- Product Managers (as presenters)
- Customer Success Leadership

Optional

- Sales Leadership
- Executive Sponsor
- Finance Leadership

Attendance should remain focused on decision-makers.

---

# Recommended Cadence

Monthly

Quarterly may be appropriate for smaller organizations.

---

# Typical Agenda

## Strategic Initiative Review

Review the organization's highest-priority initiatives.

Focus on:

- Current health
- Business value
- Customer impact
- Major accomplishments
- Significant changes

Avoid reviewing every project.

Discuss only work that materially impacts organizational objectives.

---

## Delivery Health

Review trends rather than individual sprint metrics.

Examples include:

- Portfolio health
- Delivery predictability
- Major milestones
- Cross-team dependencies
- Resource constraints

The goal is to identify systemic risks—not evaluate individual teams.

---

## Customer Outcomes

Review measurable outcomes such as:

- Customer adoption
- Customer satisfaction
- Support trends
- Customer feedback
- Business value delivered

Executives should understand the value being created—not simply what has been built.

---

## Engineering Investments

Review progress on:

- Platform modernization
- Reliability
- Performance
- Security
- Technical debt reduction

These investments should be evaluated alongside customer-facing initiatives because they increase the organization's long-term ability to deliver value.

---

## Risks and Escalations

Discuss only risks requiring executive awareness or support.

Examples include:

- Funding decisions
- Strategic tradeoffs
- Organizational constraints
- Major customer commitments
- Staffing concerns
- Cross-department dependencies

Every escalated issue should include a recommended action.

---

## Leadership Decisions

End the meeting by identifying:

- Decisions made
- Investments approved
- Risks accepted
- Follow-up actions
- Executive owners

The meeting should always produce clear direction.

# Outputs

Each Executive Review should produce:

- Executive decisions
- Updated investment priorities
- Approved organizational changes
- Leadership action items
- Escalated risks
- Portfolio adjustments

Leadership should leave with confidence in both the organization's direction and its ability to execute.

# AI Can Help

AI can assist Executive Review by:

- Summarizing portfolio health
- Drafting executive presentations
- Identifying delivery trends
- Summarizing customer feedback
- Highlighting organizational risks
- Comparing outcomes against objectives
- Generating executive dashboards

AI should reduce reporting effort so leaders can focus on strategic decisions.

---

# Common Mistakes

## Reviewing Every Project

Executives need visibility—not exhaustive detail.

Focus on initiatives that materially affect strategic objectives.

---

## Reporting Activity Instead of Outcomes

Avoid metrics such as:

- Stories completed
- Hours worked
- Story points

Instead, discuss:

- Customer value
- Business impact
- Strategic progress
- Organizational health

---

## Hiding Risks

Executive Review should surface problems early.

Leadership cannot help solve issues they do not know about.

Transparency builds trust.

---

## Treating It Like a Status Meeting

Status updates can be shared asynchronously.

Executive Review should focus on decisions, tradeoffs, and organizational priorities.

---

# Success Measures

Executive Review is successful when:

- Leadership understands the health of the Product portfolio.
- Strategic risks are identified early.
- Investment decisions are made with confidence.
- Product and Engineering present a unified strategy.
- Executives have the information needed to remove organizational blockers.

The meeting succeeds when executives leave knowing where the organization is headed, why those investments matter, and what decisions require their support.

---

# Relationship to Other Meetings

| Executive Review | Quarterly Planning |
|------------------|--------------------|
| Review current portfolio health | Plan future investments |
| Monthly | Quarterly |
| Evaluate outcomes | Define strategy |
| Executive audience | Product & Engineering leadership |

| Executive Review | Product Operations Review |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| Review business performance | Improve Product operations |
| External leadership focus | Internal Product focus |
| Strategic decisions | Process improvements |

Product Operations Review improves **how Product works**.

Executive Review demonstrates **the value Product creates**.
