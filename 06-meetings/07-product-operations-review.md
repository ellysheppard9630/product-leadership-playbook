# Product Operations Review

Product Operations Review is a recurring leadership meeting focused on improving how the Product organization operates.

Unlike Quarterly Planning, which determines what the organization will invest in, Product Operations Review evaluates how effectively the Product organization is executing its responsibilities.

The purpose is not to review products.

The purpose is to improve the Product organization itself.

---

# Purpose

Product Operations Review answers one question:

> **How can we improve the way Product operates?**

The meeting provides a dedicated opportunity to evaluate processes, metrics, tools, and team health so the organization continuously becomes more effective.

---

# Ownership

Product Operations Review is owned by Product Leadership.

Engineering leadership may participate when topics involve shared delivery processes.

The meeting focuses on improving Product as a function—not evaluating Engineering performance.

---

# Objectives

During each Product Operations Review the team should:

- Review Product performance metrics
- Evaluate discovery effectiveness
- Review backlog health across teams
- Identify process improvements
- Evaluate AI adoption
- Discuss tooling improvements
- Review documentation quality
- Identify coaching opportunities
- Share best practices across Product teams

The objective is continuous improvement.

---

# Typical Attendees

Required

- Director of Product
- Product Managers

Recommended

- Product Owners
- Engineering Managers

Optional

- UX Leadership
- QA Leadership
- Customer Success Leadership

Invite participants who contribute to improving the Product operating model.

---

# Recommended Cadence

Monthly

The meeting should focus on trends rather than individual project discussions.

---

# Typical Agenda

## Review Product Metrics

Discuss metrics such as:

- Discovery throughput
- Decision Records completed
- Customer interviews conducted
- Discovery cycle time
- Time from discovery to development
- Outcome measurement completion

Metrics should identify opportunities—not assign blame.

---

## Review Backlog Health

Evaluate:

- Discovery pipeline
- Backlog quality
- Stale initiatives
- Duplicate requests
- Upcoming delivery pipeline

Healthy backlogs create predictable delivery.

---

## Review Delivery Performance

Discuss trends such as:

- Projects delivered
- Delivery predictability
- Scope changes
- Cross-team dependencies
- Customer commitments

Focus on improving the system rather than evaluating individual teams.

---

## Review AI Adoption

Discuss:

- AI workflows being used
- Prompt libraries
- Automation opportunities
- Time saved
- Lessons learned
- New AI tools

The objective is to continuously increase Product effectiveness.

---

## Review Team Development

Discuss:

- Coaching opportunities
- Knowledge sharing
- Training needs
- Documentation improvements
- Process consistency

The Product organization should improve every month.

---

## Identify Improvement Initiatives

Select a small number of operational improvements.

Examples include:

- Improve Decision Record quality
- Reduce discovery cycle time
- Expand AI usage
- Improve roadmap visibility
- Standardize templates
- Improve customer interview process

Assign ownership before leaving the meeting.

The Product organization should become stronger after every review.

---

# AI Can Help

AI can assist by:

- Analyzing Product metrics
- Identifying process trends
- Summarizing customer feedback
- Highlighting operational bottlenecks
- Measuring AI adoption
- Drafting improvement recommendations
- Preparing leadership summaries

AI should help identify opportunities while leaving organizational decisions to leaders.

---

# Common Mistakes

## Turning It Into Project Status

Individual project discussions belong in Delivery Sync or Project Checkpoints.

This meeting focuses on improving the Product organization itself.

---

## Reviewing Individual Performance

The purpose is process improvement—not employee evaluation.

Performance conversations belong in one-on-ones.

---

## Chasing Too Many Improvements

Select a small number of meaningful operational improvements.

Continuous improvement works best through steady progress.

---

## Ignoring Data

Operational improvements should be supported by evidence.

Use metrics, customer feedback, and team observations to guide decisions.

---

# Success Measures

Product Operations Review is successful when:

- Product processes improve over time.
- Teams spend more time with customers and less time on administrative work.
- Discovery quality increases.
- AI adoption grows.
- Backlogs become healthier.
- Delivery becomes more predictable.

The meeting succeeds when the Product organization becomes more effective every month.
