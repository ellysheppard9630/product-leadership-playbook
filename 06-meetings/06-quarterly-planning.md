# Quarterly Planning

Quarterly Planning is the Product organization's strategic planning session.

Its purpose is to align Product and Engineering leadership around the initiatives, investments, and outcomes that matter most for the upcoming quarter.

Unlike Backlog Health Review, this meeting does not focus on individual backlog items.

Unlike Delivery Sync, it is not concerned with coordinating active projects.

Quarterly Planning establishes the strategic direction that guides all delivery decisions for the next quarter.

---

# Purpose

Quarterly Planning answers one question:

> **Where should we invest our time over the next quarter?**

The objective is not to build a perfect plan.

The objective is to create organizational alignment while preserving flexibility to respond to new information.

---

# Ownership

Quarterly Planning is owned by Product Leadership.

Engineering Leadership is a strategic partner throughout the planning process.

Product is responsible for:

- Business priorities
- Customer outcomes
- Roadmap direction
- Investment recommendations

Engineering is responsible for:

- Technical investment recommendations
- Capacity planning
- Platform initiatives
- Delivery considerations

The quarter should be planned together.

---

# Objectives

During Quarterly Planning the team should:

- Review company objectives
- Review roadmap initiatives
- Balance customer and technical investments
- Confirm major customer commitments
- Review engineering initiatives
- Identify major dependencies
- Evaluate organizational capacity
- Define quarterly outcomes

The goal is alignment—not detailed scheduling.

---

# Recommended Cadence

Quarterly

Schedule the meeting before the beginning of each planning cycle.

---

# Typical Agenda

## Review Business Objectives

Begin by reviewing:

- Company goals
- Department objectives
- Strategic themes
- Customer commitments

Every investment should support a larger organizational objective.

---

## Review Product Roadmap

Review:

- Current initiatives
- Discovery pipeline
- Customer demand
- Competitive landscape
- Market opportunities

Determine whether roadmap priorities remain aligned with business goals.

---

## Review Engineering Investments

Engineering presents:

- Platform modernization
- Technical debt
- Security initiatives
- Infrastructure improvements
- Performance initiatives
- Architecture evolution

Technical investments should compete alongside customer-facing work.

---

## Review Capacity

Discuss:

- Team capacity
- Planned hiring
- Vacations
- Major support commitments
- Operational work
- Planned maintenance

Capacity should influence planning—but not dictate strategy.

---

## Balance the Portfolio

Evaluate the overall investment mix.

Examples include:

- Customer enhancements
- Platform modernization
- Innovation
- Security
- Technical debt
- Regulatory work
- Operational improvements

A healthy portfolio supports both immediate customer value and long-term product sustainability.

---

## Define Quarterly Outcomes

Rather than committing to a list of features, define the outcomes the organization expects to achieve.

Examples include:

- Improve customer onboarding
- Reduce deployment time
- Increase API adoption
- Improve platform reliability
- Reduce support volume

Outcomes provide flexibility while maintaining strategic focus.

# Common Mistakes

## Planning Every Sprint

Quarterly Planning defines direction.

Engineering Planning defines execution.

Avoid planning work at the story level.

---

## Ignoring Engineering Investments

Technical improvements create future delivery capacity.

They deserve the same strategic consideration as customer-facing initiatives.

---

## Treating the Roadmap as a Contract

The roadmap represents today's best understanding.

Discovery throughout the quarter may change priorities.

Planning should remain adaptable.

---

## Overcommitting

Every organization has finite capacity.

Leave room for:

- Discovery
- Customer feedback
- Production issues
- Innovation
- Emerging opportunities

Flexibility is a strategic advantage.

---

# Success Measures

Quarterly Planning is successful when:

- Product and Engineering share common priorities.
- Strategic investments are clearly understood.
- Capacity aligns with planned outcomes.
- Customer and technical work are balanced.
- Teams understand what success looks like for the quarter.

The meeting succeeds when the organization leaves with a shared direction rather than a rigid project schedule.

# Relationship to Other Meetings

| Quarterly Planning | Backlog Health Review |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Set quarterly direction | Maintain weekly priorities |
| Strategic planning | Tactical planning |
| Leadership focus | Product execution |
| Portfolio decisions | Backlog decisions |

| Quarterly Planning | Delivery Sync |
|--------------------|---------------|
| Decide where to invest | Coordinate active delivery |
| Quarterly cadence | Weekly cadence |
| Long-term alignment | Short-term coordination |

Quarterly Planning defines the destination.

The remaining meetings help the organization reach it.
