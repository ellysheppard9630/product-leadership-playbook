# Delivery

Discovery helps teams decide **what** to build.

Delivery is the process of turning those decisions into valuable outcomes for customers and the business.

The goal of delivery isn't simply to complete work.

The goal is to deliver the right work, at the right time, with the right level of quality.

Successful Product organizations don't measure delivery by how many stories were completed.

They measure delivery by the value created.

---

# The Purpose of Delivery

A healthy delivery process should:

- Create predictable outcomes
- Enable Engineering autonomy
- Keep stakeholders informed
- Adapt as new information becomes available
- Deliver customer value incrementally
- Measure outcomes after release

Delivery is where Product strategy becomes reality.

---

# Delivery Principles

This playbook is built around several delivery principles.

## Ship Value Incrementally

Large releases create risk.

Smaller, incremental releases provide faster feedback, reduce uncertainty, and allow teams to adjust quickly.

Whenever possible, deliver value in stages rather than waiting for a "perfect" solution.

---

## Plans Should Evolve

Roadmaps are not contracts.

As teams learn through discovery, development, and customer feedback, priorities may change.

Effective Product organizations embrace learning rather than rigidly following outdated plans.

---

## Product Owns Priorities

Product determines:

- What gets built
- Why it matters
- When it should be prioritized

Engineering determines:

- How it is built
- How long it will take
- The technical approach
- Implementation tradeoffs

This partnership allows both organizations to focus on their strengths.

---

## Delivery Is More Than Development

Successful delivery includes:

- Discovery
- Prioritization
- Engineering
- QA
- Documentation
- Customer communication
- Rollout planning
- Measuring outcomes

Shipping code is only one part of delivering value.

---

## AI Supports Delivery

AI can remove administrative work throughout delivery.

Examples include:

- Sprint summaries
- Story drafting
- Release notes
- Risk identification
- Stakeholder updates
- Backlog organization
- Meeting summaries

AI should reduce overhead while leaving important decisions to people.

# Success Looks Like

Successful delivery means:

- Teams understand priorities.
- Engineering has the autonomy to execute.
- Stakeholders have visibility into progress.
- Customers receive value sooner.
- Product measures outcomes instead of activity.

Delivery isn't about moving work across a board.

It's about creating meaningful results for customers and the business.
