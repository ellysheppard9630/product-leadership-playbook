# Discovery

The purpose of discovery is simple:

Reduce uncertainty before committing to delivery.

Every initiative begins with assumptions.

Discovery is the process of testing those assumptions until the team has enough confidence to move forward.

Good discovery reduces wasted effort, uncovers hidden risks, and improves the quality of decisions.

It is not about creating documentation.

It is about learning.

---

# What Discovery Answers

Every discovery effort should answer five questions.

## 1. What problem are we solving?

Clearly define the problem before discussing solutions.

Avoid starting with feature requests.

Instead, identify:

- The customer problem
- The business problem
- The desired outcome

---

## 2. Who experiences the problem?

Not every customer has the same needs.

Understand:

- Primary users
- Secondary users
- Internal stakeholders
- External stakeholders

The more specific the audience, the better the solution.

---

## 3. Why does it matter?

Every initiative should create measurable value.

Examples include:

- Increased revenue
- Reduced operational cost
- Improved customer satisfaction
- Reduced support cases
- Faster workflows
- Better reliability

If the value isn't clear, continue discovery.

---

## 4. What are the biggest unknowns?

Discovery exists to reduce uncertainty.

Unknowns may include:

- Customer behavior
- Technical feasibility
- Regulatory requirements
- Performance concerns
- Integration complexity
- Organizational impact

Understanding uncertainty helps determine which discovery track to follow.

---

## 5. How will success be measured?

Success should be defined before development begins.

Examples include:

- Adoption rate
- Task completion time
- Error reduction
- Revenue impact
- Customer satisfaction
- System performance

Without measurable outcomes, it's difficult to know whether the initiative was successful.

---

# Discovery Is Continuous

Discovery doesn't end when development begins.

As teams learn more, assumptions change.

New risks emerge.

Customer feedback evolves.

Discovery should continue throughout delivery and after release.

Every release creates new opportunities to learn.

---

# The Discovery Mindset

Effective Product teams stay curious.

They ask questions before proposing solutions.

They validate assumptions before making commitments.

They remain willing to change direction when new information becomes available.

Discovery is not about proving an idea is right.

It's about finding the best solution to the problem.
