# Dual-Track Discovery

Not every project requires the same level of Product involvement.

Treating every initiative like a customer feature slows delivery, creates unnecessary meetings, and keeps Product teams focused on administration instead of strategy.

This operating model introduces two discovery paths.

The appropriate path depends on the type of problem being solved.

---

# Why Dual-Track Discovery?

Traditional Product organizations often apply the same process to every initiative.

Every project receives:

- Customer requirements
- User stories
- Wireframes
- Product approval
- Multiple refinements
- Sprint ceremonies

While this works well for customer-facing features, it creates unnecessary overhead for technical initiatives.

Examples include:

- Platform modernization
- Infrastructure improvements
- Security enhancements
- Performance optimization
- Refactoring
- Technical debt reduction
- Framework upgrades

Engineering often understands these problems better than Product.

Requiring Product to lead discovery for every technical initiative introduces unnecessary bottlenecks.

Instead, the discovery process should match the work.

---

# The Two Discovery Tracks

## Product-Led Discovery

Product leads discovery when the initiative primarily affects customer experience or business outcomes.

Examples include:

- Customer feature requests
- New products
- Workflow improvements
- User experience changes
- Strategic initiatives
- Funded customer development
- Market opportunities

Product responsibilities include:

- Customer research
- Problem validation
- Business case
- Requirements
- UX direction
- Prioritization
- Success metrics
- Stakeholder communication

Engineering responsibilities include:

- Technical feasibility
- Estimation
- Solution options
- Architecture guidance
- Risk identification

---

## Engineering-Led Discovery

Engineering leads discovery when the primary objective is improving the platform rather than changing customer behavior.

Examples include:

- Platform modernization
- Cloud migration
- Security initiatives
- Framework upgrades
- Performance improvements
- Infrastructure work
- Reliability improvements
- Technical debt reduction

Engineering responsibilities include:

- Root cause analysis
- Technical research
- Architecture
- Implementation approach
- Risk assessment
- Estimates

Product responsibilities include:

- Business alignment
- Prioritization
- Customer impact
- Executive communication
- Success criteria

Engineering does not require Product to define detailed requirements for work where Engineering is already the domain expert.

---

# How to Choose the Right Track

Ask one question:

> **Is the biggest uncertainty business-related or technical?**

If the uncertainty is:

- Customer needs
- Market fit
- User experience
- Business value

➡️ Product should lead discovery.

If the uncertainty is:

- Architecture
- Performance
- Infrastructure
- Scalability
- Reliability
- Security

➡️ Engineering should lead discovery.

Some initiatives contain both.

Those projects begin with Product-led discovery before transitioning into Engineering-led technical discovery.

---

# Discovery Doesn't Stop

Discovery is not a phase.

Discovery continues throughout delivery.

As teams learn more they should refine:

- Scope
- Priorities
- Risks
- Assumptions
- Success metrics

Both Product and Engineering remain engaged throughout the project.

The ownership simply shifts depending on the decisions being made.

---

# AI in Discovery

AI should accelerate discovery—not replace it.

Examples include:

- Summarizing customer interviews
- Reviewing support cases
- Identifying duplicate requests
- Drafting PRDs
- Competitive analysis
- Creating meeting summaries
- Generating executive updates
- Reviewing requirements for gaps

AI reduces administrative work so Product and Engineering can spend more time solving problems.

---

# Benefits

Organizations adopting dual-track discovery typically experience:

- Faster technical initiatives
- Less unnecessary documentation
- Greater Engineering autonomy
- More Product time spent with customers
- Fewer approval bottlenecks
- Better strategic alignment
- Higher quality discovery

Most importantly, teams stop forcing every project through the same process.

Instead, they apply the right level of discovery to the problem they're trying to solve.

---

# Example Comparison

| Product-Led Discovery | Engineering-Led Discovery |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Customer feature | Platform modernization |
| New workflow | Performance optimization |
| UX redesign | Database optimization |
| Funded customer request | Framework upgrade |
| New integration | Security enhancement |
| Business process improvement | Infrastructure migration |

Neither path is "better."

They're simply optimized for different kinds of work.

The goal is to ensure Product and Engineering each spend their time where they create the most value.
